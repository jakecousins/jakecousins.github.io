Solar Panel Installation Tool

Technologies Used

  • Solidworks for CAD modeling
  • 3D plastic printer for rapid prototyping

Problem Description:

We were tasked by array technologies to take there manual slide hammer tool and make something that would be easier and less physically demanding to use. So we came up with a design that utilizes an off-the-shelf battery powered automatic tool and retrofitted it to fit our needs.


Design Features

  • Mechanical arm with adjustable clamping for panel alignment
  • Integrated angle/tilt calibration system
  • Assistive user interface for guided installation steps
  • Foldable frame for portability and quick setup


These are some pictures of their manual slide hammer tool

Our Solution:

When trying to come up with a solution for this project we immediatley thought of trying to outsource the main tool to something already on the market and modify it to fit our needs. This idea was given to us by our industry partner at the time who showed us a similar idea the company had but untimatley it was too powerful and broke the mounting brackets and clips it was used on. So we knew we needed to take a similar approach but find something that was weaker but still had the strength to do the task at hand. After some research we found the skil auto hammer. Which is a tool originally desinged for driving nails into wood. Our idea was to a new barrel barrel to allow us to use Array's proprietary slide hammer tool head with the auto hammer. We used CAD modeling and 3D printing to rapidly produce and test our prototypes and then ordered a stronger 3D printed metal version as our final product.

Demo Videos

Conclusion:

As you can see based on the videos our tool is extremly effective at its job. It is faster, more powerful, less fatigue inducing. and easier to use while also being cost effective for Array to procure and produce. Additionally if Array were to use a similar mounting system in the future that relied on rapid impacts then theoretically this tool could be retrofitted for those methods as well, assuming the new tool heads they came up with had the same thread pitch and overall length. Overrall Array and my team were very please at how well this deisgn worked.
